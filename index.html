MindGuard — boundary & abuse-recovery tools

DARVO Disarm

  • Deny → “I hear you denying my experience.”
  • Attack → “Name-calling isn’t okay.”
  • Reverse → “I won’t accept the role reversal.”

Boundary “We can continue if we stick to the topic and speak with respect. If not, I’m stepping away.”

Gaslight Detector

  • Write the exact words said.
  • List what you observed (dates, times, screenshots).
  • Note body signals (tight chest, heat, shaking).

Anchor “I’m going with the documented facts. We’ll revisit when there’s accountability.”

Guilt-Trip Blocker

“I care about you. I’m not available for that. If you want to talk calmly tomorrow at 3, I’m open.”

Safety Plan (3×3)

List 3 people, 3 safe places, 3 coping skills. Screenshot it. Share with a trusted person.

Conditions — plain-language guides

Depression

  • Persistent low mood, loss of interest, fatigue
  • Try: movement, light, routine, social check-ins
  • Get help if: thoughts of self-harm, unable to function

Anxiety Disorders

  • GAD, Panic, Social Anxiety
  • Try: breathwork, exposure in small steps, caffeine limits
  • Get help if: panic attacks or avoidance take over

PTSD

  • Intrusive memories, hypervigilance, avoidance
  • Try: grounding, body-based calming, safe routines
  • Get help if: sleep, work, or safety are impacted

Bipolar

  • Mood episodes (depression / mania / hypomania)
  • Try: strict sleep routine, mood tracking, support
  • Get help if: decreased need for sleep + risky behavior

Schizoaffective

  • Mood symptoms + psychosis features
  • Try: structure, meds adherence, stress reduction
  • Get help if: safety concerns or reality testing is hard

BPD

  • Intense emotions, fear of abandonment, impulsivity
  • Try: DBT skills (distress tolerance, wise mind)
  • Get help if: self-harm urges, relationships feel unsafe

Narcissistic Abuse

  • Cycles of idealize–devalue–discard, gaslighting
  • Try: boundaries, documentation, grey-rock
  • Get help if: isolation, financial or physical risk

Substance Battles

  • Using to numb or cope
  • Try: urge surfing, replacement rituals, supports
  • Get help if: unsafe use, withdrawal, legal/work impact

Grief & Loss

  • Waves of sadness, anger, numbness
  • Try: permission to feel, memorial rituals, connection
  • Get help if: prolonged impairment or suicidality
